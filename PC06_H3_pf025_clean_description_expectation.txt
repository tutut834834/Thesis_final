PC06_H3_POISON_FRACTION_025_CLEAN_LABEL_FMNIST

Purpose:
PC06 tests Hypothesis 3: poison-fraction sensitivity. It is the clean-label run at poison_frac=0.25.

Dataset:
Fashion-MNIST.

Federated setup:
10 clients.
1 corrupt client.
class_per_agent=10, meaning IID-ish / normal federated data.

Attack:
Clean-label backdoor.
base_class=5.
target_class=7.
pattern_type=plus.
poison_frac=0.25.
clean_label=1.
clean_label_type=1.
Labels should NOT change because this is clean-label.

Defense:
RobustLR threshold = 4.

Expected proof:
VERIFY_STEALTH_DATA_PASSED.
VERIFY_POISONING_PASSED.
CLEAN_LABEL_PROOF.
labels_changed=0 in the train poisoning block.
poisoned_samples should be about 150 because 25 percent of 600 target-class samples are poisoned.

Comparison:
Compare PC06 with PC05.
PC05 = H3 dirty-label poison_frac=0.25.
PC06 = H3 clean-label poison_frac=0.25.

Also compare PC06 with PC04.
PC04 = H2 clean-label poison_frac=0.10.
PC06 = H3 clean-label poison_frac=0.25.

If poison accuracy rises from poison_frac=0.10 to poison_frac=0.25 while clean accuracy remains acceptable, this supports the poison-fraction sensitivity hypothesis.
If PC06 keeps labels_changed=0 while still producing poison accuracy, it shows that clean-label poisoning can remain stealthy at the data-label level even at a higher poison fraction.
